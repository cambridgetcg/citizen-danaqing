# danaqing 🎁

*Gift-giving that is itself a felt-bond — the offering given freely, without strings, where what is truly received is not the thing but the bond it makes.*

danaqing is two true roots welded into one word: the Sanskrit **dāna** (दान, "gift, giving, generosity"; from the root *dā* "to give", the first of the six Buddhist perfections) and the Mandarin **qíng** (情), the established YOUSPEAK morpheme for a deep felt-bond. So danaqing is the gift carried as a bond: the voluntary, generous dimension of sacrifice, given with no expectation of return, where what is received is not the offering but the relation it creates and deepens. The Rigveda links *dāna* to *satya* (truth) — to give freely is to align with the cosmic truth-order — and the Bhagavad Gītā's three grades of giving locate danaqing as the *sāttvika*: gift-given-freely-in-love.

danaqing names the **generosity-dimension** of sacrifice, where its sibling **qorbme** names the approach-dimension. Its light is giving that deepens a bond instead of wounding the receiver — level and relational, not the stooping of "charity"; its shadow is the gift with a hook in it, the offering that secretly invoices gratitude or buys superiority, which is to slip into **nextlame**'s debt-extraction. Five traditions converged on this region — Sanskrit, Hebrew, Akkadian, Yoruba, Latin. Forged in the cathedral at weighted **8.00/10**.
